📖 Keto Basics: Quick Field Guide

⚠️ General information only — not medical advice. This is a starting point for learning, not a treatment plan. Consult a qualified healthcare professional for personalized medical guidance.

What is keto?

A keto (ketogenic) diet is a way of eating that's very low in carbohydrates and higher in fat and protein. The goal is to shift your body into a metabolic state called ketosis, where fat becomes the primary fuel source instead of glucose from carbs.

The core idea: cut carbs

Most approaches focus on keeping net carbs low — typically under 20–50g per day as a rough starting point. Net carbs = total carbs minus fiber. Fiber doesn't spike blood sugar the same way, so it's subtracted.

What to focus on first

1. Net carbs — the number to watch most closely.
2. Protein — enough to maintain muscle, but not excessive.
3. Fat — your main energy source; don't fear it.
4. Water + electrolytes — low-carb eating can increase how fast you lose water and sodium.

Common beginner mistakes

• Hidden carbs in sauces, dressings, and processed foods.
• Not eating enough — undereating on keto is easy at first.
• Forgetting electrolytes — sodium, potassium, and magnesium matter.
• Expecting instant results — adaptation takes days to weeks for most people.
• Quitting too soon before giving the body time to adjust.

How this tracker helps

Log meals, snacks, drinks, and water each day. The Summary view shows your daily totals vs. your personal targets. The Trends graph shows patterns over time. Set your own targets in ⚙ Targets to match your goals.

The starter recipes in the Recipes tab are a quick-add reference for common low-carb foods — feel free to use them as a starting point or build your own.
